Beware of links in emails. Double check the legitimacy of the sender. Links in emails can lead to malicious sites or download of malicious content.  
Request for Personal Information: They will often ask for the following: Address, DOB, Personal ID number, credit card info, bank acc info, etc. 
Pop Up Ads: Companies will not ask you to verify information through a popup add. So make sure to hover over any likes in emails and verify them. Also do not open any attachments unless you are 100% confident in the legitimacy. For banks and such call them to make sure request to verify information is real and correct. 
Actions to Take After: Call credit card companies ASAP, change passwords, run virus prevention software, update all programs and software. Periodically check your personal accounts and notify the company whose identity was being faked.
